⚡ Automatic USB Fix — do it all on the projector
📺 No laptop needed. Just plug a USB stick into the projector and tap the button above — it formats the stick and copies all 4 files for you, right here on the device.
1
Plug a USB stick into the projector
Any USB drive with at least 2 GB free. Anything already on it will be wiped, so use a spare or back it up first.
2
Tap “Automatic USB Fix” above
It formats the stick to FAT32 and downloads all 4 firmware files straight onto it — no computer, no dragging files about. Just wait for the green tick.
3
Go to Settings → System → Local Upgrade
With the USB still plugged in, open Settings on the projector, go to System, then tap Local Upgrade (also shown as System Update → Local Update). Do NOT tap Online Update — that reinstalls the broken firmware.
4
Tap Upgrade and wait
It'll find the files on the USB and update itself — takes about 5–10 minutes. Keep the USB plugged in and don't power it off during the update.
Prefer to do it manually? Download the 4 files
⬇ bootloader.img ⬇ emmc_boot.bin ⬇ IPL_PRE.bin ⬇ DY_8D196_026.bin
Copy all 4 files to the root of a FAT32 USB stick (not in a subfolder), then follow steps 3–4 above.

💡 No computer? You can format the stick on the projector itself: Settings → Storage, select the USB drive, then Format / Erase (choose FAT32 if asked). Then download the 4 files above straight onto it.
⚠️ Important: always use Local Upgrade — never Online Update. Once this is done, please don't update your projector again until I've spoken to XBJ about why Google Play went missing. I'll let you know on TikTok as soon as it's safe.
